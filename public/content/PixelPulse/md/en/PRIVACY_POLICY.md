# Privacy Policy for Pixel Pulse

**Last Updated:** March 17, 2026

Welcome to Pixel Pulse! This Privacy Policy explains how **fertwbr** ("we," "us," or "our") handles information when you
use our Pixel Pulse mobile and wearable applications (the "Service").

Our commitment is to your privacy. The Service is designed to function primarily using **local processing on your device
**, minimizing external data transmission whenever possible.

This policy also reflects compliance with Brazilian privacy regulations, including the **Lei Geral de Proteção de
Dados (LGPD)** and **Lei nº 15.211/2025 ("ECA Digital")**, which establishes additional protections for minors in
digital environments.

## 1. Information We Process

### a) Audio Data (Microphone Access)

**Purpose:**

The core functionality of the Service requires access to your device's microphone to measure ambient sound pressure
levels (decibels).

**Processing:**

Audio signals are processed **in real-time locally on your device** in order to calculate decibel levels.

The raw audio signal is immediately discarded after the measurement is calculated.

**We do not store, record, or transmit raw audio off your device.**

**Collection Trigger:**

Microphone access may be active when:

1. You are actively using the app.
2. You enable **Background Monitoring** or **Live Sessions**.
3. Measurement is running on a connected **Wear OS** device.

A persistent notification will always be shown when background measurement is active.


### b) Session & Exposure Data (Stored Locally)

**Purpose:**

To provide charts, history, exposure analysis, and hearing safety insights.

**Data Stored:**

* timestamps
* exposure duration
* calculated decibel values (min / avg / max)
* exposure dose metrics
* analytical summaries derived from recorded sessions

**Storage:**

This information is stored **locally in a private database on your device**.

It is **not transmitted to servers operated by fertwbr**.

Users may delete this data at any time using in-app data management tools.


### c) Wear OS Synchronization

If you use the Wear OS companion application, calculated measurement data may be synchronized between your watch and
phone.

This synchronization occurs using the **Android Wearable Data Layer API**, which transfers data directly between your
personal devices via Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, or other local connectivity methods.

Raw audio is **never transferred** between devices.


### d) User-Defined Routines

The Service may allow users to configure personal routines such as **work schedules or sleep periods**.

These settings help generate contextual alerts and analytical insights.

All routine information is stored **locally on your device only** and is never transmitted to our servers.


### e) In-App Purchase Information (Pixel Pulse+)

Premium features are unlocked through a **one-time purchase** processed entirely by **Google Play Billing**.

We do not collect or store payment details.

We only receive a license validation token from Google Play to confirm purchase status.


### f) Optional Feedback, Logs, and Diagnostic Data

The app provides an **optional in-app support tool** that allows users to submit feedback.

Submitting feedback is **entirely voluntary**.

Users may choose to include:

* a contact email address
* written feedback
* screenshots or attachments
* optional diagnostic information

Diagnostic information may include technical details such as:

* device model
* Android version
* application version
* language / locale settings

This information is used solely for troubleshooting and improving the Service.

Users may edit or remove information before submitting.


## 2. Permissions

The application may request the following permissions:

* **Microphone:** Required to measure sound levels.
* **Notifications:** Used for exposure alerts and foreground service indicators.
* **Foreground Service:** Required to maintain accurate measurements while the screen is off.


## 3. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do **not** sell, rent, or share personal information with advertisers.

Since most application data is processed and stored locally on the user's device, fertwbr generally **does not have
access to user measurement data**.

Information may only be shared in the following limited circumstances:

* when a user voluntarily submits support feedback
* when required by law or valid legal request


## 4. Data Security, Retention, and Deletion

### Security

Local exposure data may be protected using encryption mechanisms supported by the Android operating system and native
libraries.

### Retention

Data is retained on the user's device for as long as the user chooses to keep it.

### System Backup Integration

Some devices may automatically include application data in **Android system backups** (for example Google Backup or
Google Drive backups).

These backups are **controlled entirely by the device operating system and the user's account settings**, not by
fertwbr.

Pixel Pulse does **not access, manage, or store these backups**.

Users can control backup behavior through their device settings.

### Deletion

Users may delete recorded exposure data at any time through the in-app data management tools.

Uninstalling the application will also remove locally stored data from the device.


## 5. Medical Disclaimer

Pixel Pulse is **not a medical device**.

The data and insights provided are informational estimates and should not be used for professional medical diagnosis or
as a substitute for professional hearing protection equipment.


## 6. Children's Privacy

The Service is not intended for children under the age of **13**.

However, the app may be accessible to teenage users depending on platform distribution settings.

In accordance with Brazilian digital protection regulations including **ECA Digital (Lei nº 15.211/2025)**, the
application is designed to minimize personal data processing and does not implement behavioral tracking or advertising
systems.


## 7. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically.

Significant changes will be communicated by updating the policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date.
